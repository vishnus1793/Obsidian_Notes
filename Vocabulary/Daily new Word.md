
## 1. **Ultracrepidarian** 

### Meaning

An **ultracrepidarian** is someone who expresses opinions on matters completely outside their area of expertise. Think of it as the ultimate term for a "know-it-all" who doesn't actually know anything about the topic at hand.

### The Simple Example

Imagine a mechanic trying to tell a surgeon how to perform heart surgery.

> _"You're doing great, but honestly, you should be using a different knot for that artery."_

The mechanic is being an **ultracrepidarian**.

## 2. **Philodox**

### **What it means:**

A person who is dogmatically in love with their own opinions.

It comes from the Greek roots _philos_ (loving) and _doxa_ (opinion or glory). Unlike a simple "know-it-all," a **philodox** isn't necessarily interested in facts, truth, or learning. They are uniquely enamored with the sound of their own beliefs, fiercely defending them simply because those beliefs belong to _them_.

## 3.**Quiddling**

_Noun / Verb_

> **Definition:** The act of wasting time by focusing on minor, trivial tasks or fussing over unnecessary details to avoid doing actual, important work.

It is the 18th-century ancestor of "procrastination," but with a specific flavor. You aren’t just lazying around watching videos; you are aggressively tidying your desk, organizing your terminal config files for the third time today, or color-coding a spreadsheet instead of writing that major report. You are staying busy, but you're **quiddling**.

### How to use it:

- _"I have a massive exam tomorrow, but instead of studying, I spent three hours quiddling with my keyboard shortcuts."_
    
- _"Stop quiddling and just commit the code already!"_

## Non-Technical (Everyday & Literary Life)

- **Word:** **Apricity**
    
- **Pronunciation:** ah-PRIS-i-tee
    
- **What it means:** The warmth of the sun in winter. It perfectly captures that specific, cozy feeling of cold air on your face while bright winter sunlight warms your skin.
    
- **Used in a sentence:** _"Stepping out of the cabin, the crisp January air was sharp, but the sheer apricity made the morning feel incredibly welcoming."_
    

## Technical Words by Field of Study

### 1. Astronomy & Physics

- **Word:** **Syzygy**
    
- **Pronunciation:** SIZ-uh-jee
    
- **What it means:** The straight-line alignment of three celestial bodies in a gravitational system (like the Sun, Earth, and Moon during an eclipse).
    
- **Used in a sentence:** _"The rare syzygy of the planets aligned perfectly, creating a spectacular alignment visible from the backyard telescope."_
    

### 2. Biology & Medicine

- **Word:** **Borborigmus**
    
- **Pronunciation:** bor-buh-RIG-muhs
    
- **What it means:** The medical term for the rumbling, gurgling noises made by the movement of fluid and gas in the intestines (aka, your stomach growling).
    
- **Used in a sentence:** _"In the middle of the silent, tense exam room, my sudden borborigmus sounded like a distant thunderclap."_
    

### 3. Psychology & Neuroscience

- **Word:** **Jamais vu**
    
- **Pronunciation:** zhah-mey VOO
    
- **What it means:** The opposite of _déjà vu_. It is the eerie feeling of experiencing a situation that you know is deeply familiar, yet it feels completely new or alien to you (like staring at a common word until it looks totally misspelled and fake).
    
- **Used in a sentence:** _"I’ve walked down this street every day for ten years, but a sudden wave of jamais vu made the entire neighborhood look completely foreign."_
    

### 4. Earth Science & Geology

- **Word:** **Petrichor**
    
- **Pronunciation:** PET-ri-kor
    
- **What it means:** The distinct, earthy scent produced when rain falls on dry soil. It is caused by the release of plant oils and a compound called geosmin into the air.
    
- **Used in a sentence:** _"After three months of intense summer drought, the first thunderstorm filled the air with a heavy, nostalgic petrichor."_
    

### 5. Philosophy & Linguistics

- **Word:** **Aporia**
    
- **Pronunciation:** uh-PAWR-ee-uh
    
- **What it means:** A state of puzzling helplessness or a logical impasse where you are faced with equally valid but completely contradictory conclusions.
    
- **Used in a sentence:** _"The deeper the philosophers dug into the paradox of time travel, the more they fell into a frustrating state of aporia."_
    

### 6. Technology & Computing

- **Word:** **Skeuomorph**
    
- **Pronunciation:** SKYOO-uh-morf
    
- **What it means:** A digital design element that mimics a physical, real-world object to make it familiar to users (like the "trash can" icon for deleting files, or a digital notebook app featuring fake leather stitching).
    
- **Used in a sentence:** _"Early smartphone software relied heavily on skeuomorphs, giving digital buttons a glossy, plastic look so people knew they could tap them."_


## **Alexithymia**

_(pronounced: ah-lek-si-THIGH-mee-ah)_

### What it means:

**Alexithymia** is the **inability to identify, name, or describe your own emotions.**

It comes from Greek roots:

- **A-** (lacking)
    
- **Lexis** (words)
    
- **Thymos** (soul or emotion)
    

Literally, it means **"having no words for emotions."**



## **Anemoia**

_(pronounced: an-eh-MOY-ah)_

### What it means:

**Anemoia** is **a deep nostalgia or longing for a time you’ve never actually known.**

It’s that distinct feeling you get when you look at old, grainy photographs from the 1920s, watch a movie set in the 1970s, or listen to a vintage track, and suddenly feel a genuine ache of homesickness for that era—even though you weren't even alive then.

It contrasts with standard nostalgia, which requires you to have personally lived through the memory. Anemoia is a trick of the imagination, where your mind builds a romanticized version of a past era and makes you miss it.
