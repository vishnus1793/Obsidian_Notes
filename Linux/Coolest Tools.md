### `bandwhich` (Terminal Bandwidth Utilization)

While `flow` gives you beautiful aggregate throughput waveforms, `bandwhich` answers the immediate follow-up question: _"What exact process is hogging my data right now?"_ It maps open sockets and traffic spikes directly to running applications.

- **Why it matches the vibe:** Written in Rust, it’s remarkably fast and utilizes crisp, minimalist responsive layouts to break down network consumers by process, connection, or remote IP.
    
- **Install (Arch):** `sudo pacman -S bandwhich`

### `gping` (Ping, but with a beautiful graph)

Instead of watching a wall of text spit out lines like `64 bytes from... time=12ms`, `gping` translates your connection latency into a live, scrolling line graph directly inside your terminal.

- **Why it matches the vibe:** It follows the exact same philosophy as `flow` — single-purpose, highly visual, and uses Braille characters to plot smooth latency spikes over time.
    
- **Install (Arch):** `sudo pacman -S gping`
